Privacy Policy

App: QuickPay v1.3  ·  Developer: no10messi.skr  ·  Last updated: March 2026

QuickPay is a non-custodial, serverless Solana payment app. We do not operate any servers, and we do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data to external services.

Overview

QuickPay allows users to send and receive SOL, USDC, USDT, and SKR (Seeker) on the Solana blockchain. Features include Solana Pay QR code generation and scanning, direct wallet-to-wallet transfers, token balance display, local transaction history with calendar view, receipt image generation, and local history backup. All transactions occur directly on the Solana network. QuickPay never holds, controls, or has access to user funds or private keys.

Data Collection

QuickPay collects no personal information. We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, location, or any other identifying information. No account or registration is required.

Local Data Storage

QuickPay stores the following data locally on your device only (via AsyncStorage). This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to any server:

  • Wallet connection info: Your public key (wallet address), MWA auth token, and wallet URI for reconnecting to the same wallet without re-authorization.
  • Transaction history: Records of payments you have sent or received through the app, including transaction signatures, amounts, timestamps, and any personal notes you have added to individual transactions.
  • Quick amount presets: User-configured quick-select amount values.
  • PIN settings: If you enable the optional PIN lock, a hashed PIN is stored locally for device-level app security.

You can clear all locally stored data at any time by clearing the app's data through your device settings or by disconnecting your wallet within the app.

File Storage (On-Device)

QuickPay may write the following files directly to your device storage upon explicit user action. These files are not transmitted to any server:

  • Receipt images: When you tap "Save to Gallery", a PNG receipt image is saved to Pictures/QuickPay/ on your device. This requires storage write permission.
  • History backup files: A JSON file containing your local transaction history (including any personal notes) is saved to Downloads/QuickPay/. This happens when you tap the backup button manually, or automatically once every 24 hours when the History screen is opened. A maximum of 3 backup files are kept; the oldest is deleted automatically when a new one is created. These files can be restored into the app at any time from the same folder.

These files remain entirely on your device. You can delete them at any time using your device's file manager.

Camera Permission

QuickPay requests camera access solely to scan QR codes for payment processing. The camera is used only within the QR scanner screen. No images or video are recorded, stored, or transmitted. Camera access can be revoked at any time through your device settings.

Private Keys & Wallet Security

QuickPay does not access your private keys or seed phrase. All cryptographic signing is handled exclusively by the Solana Mobile Seed Vault, a secure hardware element on your device. QuickPay communicates with the Seed Vault through the Mobile Wallet Adapter (MWA) protocol, which never exposes private key material to the application.

For security, QuickPay restricts wallet connections exclusively to the Seed Vault. Third-party wallet apps (e.g., Phantom, Solflare) are not permitted to authorize transactions within QuickPay. This restriction is enforced at the application level to prevent unauthorized transaction signing.

Token Security

QuickPay validates incoming SPL token payments against a hardcoded whitelist of approved mint addresses (SOL, USDC, USDT, SKR). Payments involving unrecognized token mint addresses or mismatched token program owners are automatically rejected by the app. This measure protects users from accepting fraudulent or scam tokens.

Blockchain Transactions

When you send or receive a payment, the transaction is broadcast directly to the Solana blockchain. Blockchain transactions are public by nature. Transaction history displayed within the app is stored locally on your device and also available on the public Solana ledger.

No Servers

QuickPay operates without a backend server. The app communicates directly with Solana RPC nodes solely to submit and confirm transactions, fetch token balances, and monitor incoming payments.

Third-Party Services

QuickPay does not integrate any advertising, analytics, or tracking services. The app communicates with the following external services solely for blockchain operations and price data:

  • Solana RPC endpoints: Public endpoints (PublicNode, Helius, Solana Mainnet) for submitting transactions, fetching balances, and confirming payments. No personal data is transmitted.
  • Jupiter Price API (api.jup.ag): Primary source for real-time SOL/USD and SKR/USD price data.
  • CoinGecko API: Fallback price data source used only when Jupiter is unavailable.
  • Solana Explorer (explorer.solana.com): The Receipt screen loads the Explorer's transaction receipt view in a WebView to display confirmed transaction details (sender, recipient, amount, fees, status). No personal data is transmitted; only the transaction signature is included in the URL.

No personal data is transmitted to any of these services. The USD amounts and token quantity estimates shown in the app are approximate reference values derived from market price data and may differ from actual on-chain amounts. The app displays an "≈" symbol and a disclaimer to indicate that values are estimates.

Notifications

QuickPay may display local notifications to alert you when an incoming payment is confirmed. These notifications are generated entirely on-device. No push notification service or external server is used.

Children's Privacy

QuickPay does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, including children under the age of 13.
